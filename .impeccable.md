## Design Context

### Users

People who want to understand a personal life or a public biography as a finite, week-by-week timeline. They use the page to render a birth date or sourced profile, read stages and events, and save a useful image.

### Brand Personality

Reflective, editorial, and quietly precise. The experience should feel contemplative without becoming solemn, and data-rich without reading like a dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction

Keep the established KISKIR design system: warm paper background, Georgia/Avenir typography, black-and-cream controls, Pastel Bloom stage colors, native disclosure triangles, and the existing week-grid language. Improve composition through hierarchy, alignment, and whitespace rather than a new visual theme.

### Design Principles

- Make the week grid the unmistakable primary artifact.
- Use one consistent explanatory axis for controls, metadata, and editing tools.
- Keep creation tools available but visually downstream from the finished visualization.
- Preserve honest date precision, readable exports, and local-first behavior.
- Prefer compact editorial grouping over repeated full-width bands or nested cards.

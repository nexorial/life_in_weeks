# Steve Jobs

## Status

Review draft for the first public-figure Life in Weeks profile.

## Agency Agents Line

Selected line:

> He returned, simplified the company, and made phones feel inevitable.

Other usable lines from the marketing-agent pass:

- Adopted in California, he turned counterculture curiosity into products.
- Apple rose fast; exile proved vision could outpace diplomacy.
- Pixar and NeXT kept him building while Apple lost direction.
- Illness shortened the runway; death left the timeline unfinished.

## Verification Notes

- Some well-known dates have source disagreement at day precision. The Apple departure is represented as `1985-09-17`, matching the resignation-letter date in the Wikipedia chronology; some retrospectives use September 16 for the board/exit moment.
- The Reed, India/Atari, Apple II public introduction, cancer diagnosis, and liver transplant entries use approximate machine dates with visible `dateLabel` values where the source confidence is year/month rather than exact day.
- This profile intentionally separates verified source facts from interpretive stage labels. The stage labels are editorial grouping, not claims that only one activity happened in that period.

## Verified Chronology

| Date | Event | Validation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1955-02-24 | Born in San Francisco; adopted shortly afterward by Paul and Clara Jobs. | Wikipedia; Stanford commencement text |
| 1971 | Meets Steve Wozniak; the two later sell blue boxes. | Computer History Museum |
| 1972 | Enters Reed College and drops out after about six months. | Stanford commencement text; Wikipedia |
| 1974 | Works at Atari and travels through India seeking spiritual direction. | Wikipedia; Stanford commencement context |
| 1976-04-01 | Co-founds Apple Computer Company with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. | Library of Congress; Wikipedia |
| 1977-04 | Apple II is introduced to the public. | Smithsonian; Computer History Museum |
| 1980-12-12 | Apple goes public; Jobs becomes wealthy at age 25. | Computer History Museum; Morgan Stanley history |
| 1984-01-24 | Macintosh is introduced. | Wikipedia; contemporaneous Apple history references |
| 1985-09-17 | Jobs submits resignation after a power struggle with Apple leadership. | Wikipedia |
| 1985 | Founds NeXT after leaving Apple. | Wikipedia |
| 1986-02-03 | Purchases Lucasfilm's Computer Division and forms Pixar as an independent company. | Pixar; Wikipedia |
| 1988-10-12 | Unveils the NeXT Computer in San Francisco. | Wikipedia; contemporary-retrospective reporting |
| 1991-03-18 | Marries Laurene Powell. | Wikipedia |
| 1995-11-22 | Toy Story opens; Pixar goes public a week later. | Pixar; Wikipedia |
| 1996-12-20 | Apple announces agreement to acquire NeXT, bringing Jobs back. | Apple NeXT acquisition press release PDF |
| 1997-09-16 | Jobs is formally named interim CEO of Apple. | Wikipedia; Steve Jobs Archive timeline |
| 1998-05-06 | Unveils the iMac, the first major product of his second Apple era. | Wikipedia iMac G3; AppleInsider retrospective |
| 2000-01-05 | Drops the interim title and becomes permanent Apple CEO. | SFGate; Wired; Wikipedia |
| 2001-10-23 | Apple introduces the iPod. | Apple Newsroom |
| 2003-04-28 | Apple launches the iTunes Music Store. | Apple Newsroom |
| 2003-10 | Diagnosed with a rare pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor. | Wikipedia; medical-source summaries |
| 2004-08-01 | Announces successful surgery to remove the pancreatic tumor. | Daring Fireball copy of Jobs email |
| 2005-06-12 | Gives Stanford commencement address. | Stanford Report |
| 2006-05-05 | Disney completes Pixar acquisition; Jobs joins Disney's board. | Disney press release |
| 2007-01-09 | Introduces the iPhone at Macworld. | Apple Newsroom |
| 2009-03 | Receives a liver transplant in Tennessee. | AMA Journal of Ethics; CBS/AP reporting |
| 2010-01-27 | Introduces the iPad. | Apple Newsroom; Smithsonian |
| 2011-08-24 | Resigns as Apple CEO; Tim Cook is named CEO and Jobs becomes chairman. | Apple Newsroom |
| 2011-10-05 | Dies in Palo Alto at age 56. | Apple; PBS NewsHour; Wikipedia |
